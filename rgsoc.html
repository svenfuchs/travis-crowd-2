Rails Girls Summer of Code

Travis Foundation managed and supported Rails Girls Summer of Code as their first community program. Especially Sven Fuchs and Anika Lindtner helped run and manage the initiative over the summer. Our goal is to make sure this to was only the first year of many more for a Rails Girls summer of Code to come.

The program

Rails Girls Summer of Code funds women around the world to work full-time over summer on open-source software projects. Modelled after Google Summer of Code, but with a focus on beginners and women wanting to go into programming full-time. The initiative aims to get women involved in Open Source. It is the first ever event of its kind in the world. It began 1st of July, 2013, and ran for three months.

The teams

19 sponsored and 10 volunteering students took part in the program. They worked from all over the world on Open Source projects like Sinatra, Farm Subsidy Open Government Data, Hackety Hack, Rails, Rubinius, Diaspora, Bundler, Species+, impress.js and more!
